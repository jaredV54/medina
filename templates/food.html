Favorite Food Delicacies

Sisig

Sisig is a popular Kapampangan dish made from chopped pig’s face and ears, seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili peppers. It originated from Pampanga and is known for its crispy texture and savory-tangy flavor. I love it because it reminds me of festive family gatherings and celebrations at home.

Adobo

Adobo is a classic Filipino dish made by simmering meat, usually chicken or pork, in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and bay leaves. It’s a comfort food for many Filipinos, including me, because it brings back memories of home-cooked meals and cozy weekends with my family.

Creamy Beef with Mushroom

This dish combines tender slices of beef with a creamy mushroom sauce, often served over rice or pasta. It’s rich, comforting, and easy to prepare. I enjoy making it when I want something hearty and satisfying.

Sinigang

Sinigang is a sour soup made with tamarind and various vegetables, often paired with pork, shrimp, or fish. Its tangy flavor makes it unique and refreshing, especially on rainy days. I love it because it’s my mom’s specialty, and it reminds me of her warm, nurturing cooking.

BBQ

Filipino-style barbecue features skewered marinated pork or chicken grilled over charcoal, often served with vinegar dipping sauce. It's a favorite during street fairs and family picnics. I love the smoky-sweet taste and the fun memories of eating BBQ with friends after school.
